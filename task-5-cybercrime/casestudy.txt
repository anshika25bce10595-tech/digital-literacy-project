CASE STUDY: UPI / Online Payment FRAUD

UPI frauds are fraudulent activities where scammers use deceptive tactics—like phishing, fake QR codes, or impersonation—to trick users into authorizing unauthorized money transfers or sharing sensitive PINs/OTPs through the Unified Payments Interface. These scams, often called digital payment fraud, phishing scams, or social engineering attacks, aim to directly debit funds from a victim's bank account.
Typically, the fraud happens step-by-step as follows: first, the fraudster approaches the victim (often a shopkeeper or individual) and initiates a fake transaction using a UPI app. Second, instead of completing the payment, they manipulate or edit a screenshot showing a successful payment. Third, they display this fake proof to the victim or send it via messaging apps. Fourth, trusting the confirmation, the victim hands over goods or assumes payment is received. In other cases, fraudsters may send malicious links or payment requests, gaining access to the victim’s account and transferring money without consent.
The most common targets are small shopkeepers, street vendors, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments, as they may not verify transactions carefully. Sometimes even educated users fall victim due to urgency or lack of awareness.
The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, mental stress, and time-consuming legal procedures. Victims may struggle to recover money, and in business cases, repeated fraud can lead to significant losses. Overall, UPI fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness, verifying transactions in bank apps, and never trusting screenshots or unknown payment requests.
